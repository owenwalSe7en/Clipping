## 3. PROFILE AESTHETIC RECOMMENDATIONS

### COLOR PALETTE
**Primary:** Deep Navy (#001F3F) or Charcoal (#36454F)  
**Secondary:** Electric Gold (#FFB700) or Crimson Red (#DC143C)  
**Accent:** White or Light Gray (contrast for readability)

**Rationale:**
- Deep colors signal seriousness, ambition, premium positioning (not bright/playful)
- Gold/red accents create urgency and energy (calls to action, highlights)
- High contrast ensures profile pic and graphics pop on mobile
- Aligns with "alpha" aesthetic popular in this demographic

### PROFILE PICTURE STYLE
**Recommended:** One of the following
1. **Bold typography** — Your handle or one power word ("EDGE", "GRIT", "VICTOR") in the gold/red color on dark background, centered
2. **Silhouette with symbol** — Minimalist silhouette of head/shoulders with a small icon (lightning bolt, crown, upward arrow) overlay in gold
3. **Photo + text blend** — If using a personal photo: clean headshot with text overlay of your handle in gold (if face reveals; keep it minimal and professional)
4. **Abstract geometric** — Overlapping shapes/arrows pointing upward in your accent color on dark background (AI-generated or Canva-designed)

**Why not a personal photo at launch?**
- Brand-first positioning feels more scalable (works across multiple campaign partnerships)
- Allows repurposing as you hand off management later
- More memorable than generic face (design is sticky)

### INSTAGRAM HIGHLIGHT COVERS
**Use these 6 covers (one per main content pillar):**

1. **Mindset** — Brain icon or upward arrow in gold on navy
2. **Grind** — Barbell or hustle symbol in gold on navy
3. **Money** — Dollar sign or growth chart in gold on navy
4. **Fitness** — Dumbell or fire emoji in gold on navy
5. **Wisdom** — Book or light bulb in gold on navy
6. **Community** — People/network symbol in gold on navy

**Design specs:** 1080x1350px, navy background, gold icon, 3-5px white border for separation from main feed

### OVERALL VISUAL IDENTITY SUMMARY
| Element | Specification |
|---------|---|
| **Font** | Sans-serif, bold weight (Montserrat, Inter Bold, or similar) |

## 8. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC OPTIMIZATION NOTES

### TikTok
- **Hook in first 1-2 seconds** (attention span = brutal)
- **Use captions on-screen** (even though audio exists; many watch muted)
- **End with a question** (drives comments, which boosts algorithmic reach)
- **Test posting times:** 7-9am (commute), 12-1pm (lunch), 6-8pm (evening wind-down)
- **Trending sounds optional** (your niche is wisdom/mindset, not music-driven)
- **Engage with similar creators daily** (3-5 min/day; like, comment, follow strategically)

### Instagram
- **Caption length matters** — aim for 100-150 chars (sweet spot for mobile, not overwhelming)
- **First line above the break is critical** (use your hook, emoji, or number)
- **Captions should feel like a friend wrote them** (not corporate)
- **Use Stories for engagement** (behind-the-scenes, mini-polls, share rate alerts)
- **Repost top TikToks with IG Reels** (max 15-30 sec; IG's native short-form)
- **Post 1x/day, max 2x/day** (IG algorithm penalizes over-posting)

### YouTube Shorts
- **Standalone channel or existing channel?** Start standalone (easier to test, pivot if needed)
- **Description matters** (include link to Linktree with bounty platforms/campaigns)
- **End screens & cards** (link to older Shorts or full-length videos if available)
- **Captions/subtitles mandatory** (YouTube favors accessibility; improves discoverability)
- **Thumbnail quality** (custom thumbnails > auto-generated for YouTube Shorts)
- **Post 2-3x/week** (YouTube rewards consistency without penalizing)

## 10. QUICK REFERENCE: TONE & VOICE

### DO:
- Be direct, unfiltered, honest (young men respect authenticity over polish)
- Use short sentences and line breaks (mobile-first formatting)
- Reference real people (Naval, Arnold, Huberman, etc.) not generic advice
- Create slight tension/challenge in captions (the stinging question, the counterintuitive flip)
- Be specific (numbers, timelines, actual examples vs. vague motivation)

### DON'T:
- Use clichés ("never give up," "you got this," "believe in yourself") without context
- Be corporate or salesy (no "we're excited to announce")
- Overuse emojis (3-4 max per caption; emoji = emphasis, not decoration)
- Ask rhetorical questions without a real answer or discussion prompt
- Pretend to be an expert if you're curating (lean into "here's what smart people know")
- Post motivational content that doesn't connect to action (motivation without principle = waste)

### EXAMPLE TONE COMPARISON

**Too generic:**
```
You can do anything you set your mind to! 💪 Just believe! #NeverGiveUp
```

**Right tone:**
```
You can't believe your way through a workout.
You have to show up and do the reps.

That's what separates the talkers from the builders.
```

## 11. COMMON MISTAKES TO AVOID

1. **Mixing vibes inconsistently** — Don't post philosophy at 8am then fitness memes at 12pm. Keep energy consistent.
2. **Chasing trends over positioning** — Every trending sound ≠ worth your time. Protect the niche.
3. **Neglecting comments** — Algorithm notices if your comments are hollow. Write real replies.
4. **Over-selling campaigns** — Campaign clips work when they feel organic (see Pillar 6 framing).
5. **Ignoring analytics** — Don't guess. Track what works, double down on winners.
6. **Posting sporadically** — 1 clip/day for 7 days beats 7 clips then silence. Consistency is the meta.
7. **Using stock photos/generic templates** — Custom design + real content = trust. Use Canva, but keep it branded.
8. **Ghosting your audience** — New followers check your recent comments. Be there in the first 24hrs.
