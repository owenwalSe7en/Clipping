# Curated Content Sources — Refined Watchlist
## A Mindset/Self-Improvement Instagram Clip Page for Young Men 18-30

---

## How This List Was Built

This watchlist was synthesized from three comprehensive research streams:

1. **Creator Audit** — Analyzed 30+ potential content sources against the core filter: *"Would this feel at home on @value's (5.6M follower mindset page) feed?"* Removed pure entertainment creators (gaming streamers, spectacle-based content), kept only wisdom/education/vulnerability-driven sources.

2. **Market Study** — Examined how large financial Instagram pages (15M+ followers) actually source content. Primary pattern: they clip from podcasts, interviews, keynotes, and news—not standalone YouTube creators.

3. **Supply Chain Analysis** — Reverse-engineered @value's actual clipping sources: long-form podcasts (Joe Rogan, Tim Ferriss), shareholder meetings (Buffett), historical keynotes (Steve Jobs), and interview archives.

**Key Finding:** The best content sources are NOT individual YouTube channels, but rather **long-form interview formats, public archives, and historical speeches** where viral moments are naturally embedded in 2-3+ hour videos. These formats create the most clippable content: authentic vulnerability, structured wisdom frameworks, and quotable breakthroughs.

---

## Source Categories — Five Types of Clippable Content

### 1. **Individual Creators**
YouTubers and media personalities with consistent educational/wisdom content. Minimum bar: explicit philosophy, teachable frameworks, or authentic vulnerability. NO entertainment-first spectacle.

### 2. **Podcasts & Interview Shows**
Long-form interview formats (2-3 hour episodes) where viral moments emerge naturally. These are the **primary goldmine** for clipping—each episode contains 4-8 extractable moments.

### 3. **Keynotes, Speeches & Conferences**
Public talks, shareholder meetings, conference presentations. Often free/public domain or fair-use clippable. Warren Buffett shareholder meetings, TED Talks, CEO keynotes.

### 4. **Historical & Archival Content**
Past speeches, interviews, and moments from before creators had "personal brands." Steve Jobs 1997, Bill Gates early talks, CEO shareholder letters. Powerful for comparison clips ("10 years later") and "aged like wine/milk" formats.

### 5. **Case Studies & Business Stories**
Narrative clips of business failures, pivots, and comebacks. Blockbuster collapse, BlackBerry decline, WeWork implosion, founder origin stories. "Lessons from failure" content resonates heavily with 18-30 males.

## Clipping Risk Tiers — Guidance for Automated Publishing

### Tier 1 (LOW RISK — Clip Freely)

**Characteristics:**
- Creators explicitly encourage clipping
- Have official clips channels
- Educational positioning (fair use is strongest)
- Public domain or shareholder/public talks
- Creator policy explicitly allows clips

**Sources in Tier 1:**
- Andrew Huberman (official clips channel)
- Jocko Willink (encourages distribution)
- Ryan Holiday (stoicism is "open source")
- Jason Calacanis (built brand on quotes)
- Tim Ferriss (designed for thoughtful distribution)
- Steven Bartlett (official clips channel)
- Joe Rogan (extensive clips ecosystem exists)
- Lex Fridman (official clips channel)
- Mike Tyson (clips channel)
- Warren Buffett (public shareholder meetings)
- Steve Jobs (public speeches)
- Shareholder letters (public documents)

**Publishing Strategy:**
- Clip freely with standard Instagram formatting
- No need for elaborate "fair-use commentary" overlays
- Attribution helps but not legally required for educational content

### Tier 2 (MEDIUM RISK — Use Fair-Use Framing)

**Characteristics:**
- Copyrighted content but clipping is common industry practice
- Can be clipped if framed with educational overlay/commentary
- Requires thoughtful context (not just extraction)
- Risk manageable with attribution + fair-use justification

**Sources in Tier 2:**
- Joe Rogan Experience (Spotify copyright, but clips are industry standard)
- The Tim Ferriss Show (copyrighted, but creator-friendly)
- How I Built This (NPR copyright, educational framing helps)
- TED Talks from some speakers (license required for commercial use)
- Some keynotes with corporate copyright holders
- David Goggins (copyright protective, but clips with motivational framing exist)

**Publishing Strategy:**
- Add text overlay with main insight/lesson
- Include brief commentary or context (not just raw clip)
- Credit source and creator
- Frame as educational/motivational analysis
- Example: "Warren Buffett on X — Here's the full breakdown..." (not just clip + music)

### Tier 3 (HIGH RISK — Avoid or Be Very Strategic)

**Characteristics:**
- Entertainment-first copyright holders (aggressive protection)
- Creators who actively take down clips
- Livestream content with licensing issues
- Content where fair use is weaker
- Creators with explicit anti-clipping policies

**Sources to Avoid:**
- MrBeast (entertainment spectacle, protective copyright)
- Kai Cenat (gaming livestreams, complex copyright)
- Music-heavy content without licenses
- Premium subscription-only content (Netflix exclusives, etc.)
- Creators who've shown pattern of DMCA takedowns

**If You Must Clip (Very Selective Cases):**
- Transform heavily (multiple sources, deep analysis)
- Fair-use commentary must dominate (not just clip + overlay)
- Credit source generously
- Be prepared for takedown requests
- Only for exceptionally high-value moments

## Implementation Notes for Clippers

### Archive Building Strategy
- **YouTube channel subscriptions:** Subscribe to all primary sources' YouTube channels for notification alerts on new uploads
- **Podcast app subscriptions:** Use Apple Podcasts or Spotify apps for episode notifications
- **Download tools:** Use tools like `yt-dlp` (for YouTube) or podcast download services to maintain offline archives (important for archival content that might disappear)

### Clipping Workflow
1. **Identify source:** Match incoming content to one of the five categories
2. **Risk assessment:** Check if Tier 1/2/3 and adjust frame accordingly
3. **Extract moment:** 15-90 seconds of core insight (adjust per platform guidelines)
4. **Add framing:** Text overlay with key insight or question for engagement
5. **Credit:** Always tag source, creator, and platform in caption

### Platform-Specific Notes
- **Instagram Reels:** 15-90 seconds (aim for 45 seconds as sweet spot)
- **TikTok:** Same, but lean into trends/sounds more aggressively
- **YouTube Shorts:** Up to 60 seconds (full captions help)
- **Caption strategy:** Lead with the hook (biggest insight), not a teaser

### Red Flags to Avoid
- Avoid clipping during creator's own podcast/streamed clips (redundant)
- Avoid personal drama unrelated to mindset teaching
- Avoid politics/culture war unless explicitly framed as business/mindset lesson
- Avoid overusing same creator (rotate sources weekly to avoid algorithmic penalty)

Before you start, answer:

1. **Platform distribution:** Where are you primarily posting? (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, all three?)
2. **Posting frequency:** How many clips per day/week are you aiming for?

4. **Copyright policy:** What's your tolerance for copyright risk? (Tier 1 only vs. willing to use Tier 2 with fair-use framing?)
